Bernoulli's Principle

Bernoulli's principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid occurs simultaneously with a decrease in pressure or a decrease in the fluid's potential energy.

In this visualization:

  • Observe how particles move faster in the narrow section.
  • The color gradient indicates pressure: blue for high, red for low.
  • Adjust the sliders to see how speed and tube width affect the flow.

This principle explains phenomena like lift in airplane wings and the curve of a baseball.
