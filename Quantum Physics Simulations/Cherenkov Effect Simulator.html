Cherenkov Effect Simulator

Observe the Cherenkov effect as charged particles move faster than light through a medium, creating a characteristic blue glow.

Key Concepts:

  • Particle Speed vs. Light Speed: In this medium, light travels at a reduced speed. When a charged particle exceeds this speed, it produces Cherenkov radiation.
  • Emission of Cherenkov Radiation: As the particle moves faster than light in the medium, it creates an electromagnetic shock wave, visible as a blue glow.
  • Cherenkov Cone: The angle of the light cone (θ) is related to the particle's speed (v) and the speed of light in the medium (c) by the formula: cos(θ) = c/v
  • Particle Speed Effect: As the particle's speed increases, the cone angle widens, and the intensity of the blue glow increases.
